# Debo PR Action Plan 2026/27 — Solo Edition

**Rotaract Club of Debo | District 9212 | Rotary Year 2026/27**

A realistic, one-person execution plan for the official PR Action Plan, scoped for **3–5 hours per week**, with team-expansion milestones built in. Every task includes a free learning resource.

---

## Your Weekly Rhythm (3–5 hrs)

Split your week into three short sessions. Consistency beats intensity.

| Session | Time | Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | ~1 hr | **Learn** — one lesson from your current task's resource |
| 2 | ~2 hrs | **Create** — batch-produce posts, designs, captions, edits |
| 3 | ~1 hr | **Publish + Engage** — schedule posts, reply to comments, log metrics |

**Key solo adjustments from the original plan:**
- 2 posts/week instead of 3 (scale up when you have help)
- #MeetDebo is monthly, not weekly
- The "documentary" becomes a 2–3 minute highlights reel
- Team expansion steps are built into Q2 and Q3

---

## Prep — Early July 2026

*Set up your systems before the year starts.*

### 1. Create your PR HQ — Jul 1–7 (2–3 hrs)
Set up a Google Drive folder structure (Photos, Designs, Reports, Templates) and a simple content calendar spreadsheet. Everything you make this year lives here — it becomes your handover pack later.
> Learn: Hootsuite — How to Create a Social Media Content Calendar
> https://blog.hootsuite.com/how-to-create-a-social-media-content-calendar/

### 2. Download official Rotaract brand assets — Jul 1–7 (1–2 hrs)
Get the official Rotaract logos, colors, and fonts from the Rotary Brand Center. Correct branding is the fastest way to look professional and is required for district visibility.
> Learn: Rotary Brand Center (free with a My Rotary account)
> https://brandcenter.rotary.org/

### 3. Learn Canva fundamentals — Jul 1–14 (3–4 hrs total)
Canva will be your main design tool all year. Complete the free Canva Essentials course before Q1 design work begins.
> Learn: Canva Design School (free courses)
> https://www.canva.com/design-school/

---

## Q1 — July–September 2026

*Foundation: brand, audit, and a sustainable posting rhythm.*

### 4. Run a platform audit — Jul 8–21 (3–4 hrs)
List every club account (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok). Record follower counts, last post date, password holders, and bio quality. Fix bios, links, and profile photos as you go.
> Learn: Buffer — How to Perform a Social Media Audit
> https://buffer.com/library/social-media-audit/

### 5. Choose 2 primary platforms — Jul 22–28 (1 hr)
You cannot run 4 platforms well alone. Pick Instagram plus one more (based on your audit) as primary; others get cross-posts only. Expand when the team grows.
> Learn: Hootsuite — How to Create a Social Media Strategy
> https://blog.hootsuite.com/how-to-create-a-social-media-marketing-plan/

### 6. Brand refresh: build your template kit — Aug 1–31 (6–8 hrs total)
In Canva, create a brand kit (colors, fonts, logo) and 4–5 reusable post templates: event announcement, event recap, #MeetDebo spotlight, quote card, and story template. This makes every future post a 15-minute job.
> Learn: Canva — Brand Kits and Templates course
> https://www.canva.com/design-school/courses/

### 7. Write a 1-page PR strategy — Aug 15–31 (2–3 hrs)
One page: audience, 3–4 content pillars (service projects, members, Hidden Ethiopia, club life), posting cadence, and the follower target (1,262 → 1,800+). Share it with the club board.
> Learn: Meta Blueprint (free social marketing courses)
> https://www.facebook.com/business/learn

### 8. Launch #MeetDebo — monthly member spotlight — Sep 2026, then monthly (2 hrs/month)
Start monthly (not weekly — you're solo). Create a 5-question interview form, get a photo of one member, and publish with your spotlight template. Batch 2–3 interviews at each club meeting.
> Learn: Later — Instagram Marketing Guide
> https://later.com/blog/instagram-marketing/

### 9. Start the 2-posts-per-week rhythm — Sep 1, 2026 onward (2 hrs/week)
Begin consistent posting: 2 quality posts per week on your primary platforms. Use Meta Business Suite (free) to batch-schedule a week in one sitting.
> Learn: Meta Business Suite — Scheduling Posts
> https://www.facebook.com/business/tools/meta-business-suite

### 10. Record baseline metrics — Sep 20–30 (1 hr)
Log current followers, average likes, reach, and engagement in a simple sheet. Update monthly — it becomes the backbone of your Q4 report.
> Learn: Later — Instagram Analytics explained
> https://later.com/blog/instagram-analytics/

## Q2 — October–December 2026

*Content engine: writing, video, press kit — and your first recruit.*

### 11. Learn copywriting basics — Oct 1–31 (1 hr/week)
Better captions = more engagement. Take Google's free digital marketing fundamentals course (content/social modules) and practice on your weekly posts.
> Learn: Google Digital Garage — Fundamentals of Digital Marketing (free, certified)
> https://learndigital.withgoogle.com/digitalgarage/course/digital-marketing

### 12. Build the Oct–Dec content calendar — Oct 1–10 (2 hrs)
Map every known club event, Rotary observance (e.g. World Polio Day, Oct 24), and #MeetDebo slot into your calendar sheet. Planning ahead is what makes 3–5 hrs/week enough.
> Learn: Rotary — Calendar of Special Observances
> https://my.rotary.org/en/news-media/calendar

### 13. Create the club press kit — Nov 1–30 (4–5 hrs total)
A single polished PDF: club history, mission, key projects (Hidden Ethiopia!), impact numbers, leadership contacts, and logos. This unlocks sponsorships and media coverage.
> Learn: Prezly — How to Create a Press Kit
> https://www.prezly.com/academy/how-to-create-a-press-kit

### 14. Learn phone videography + CapCut editing — Oct–Nov (1 hr/week for 6 weeks)
Learn to shoot steady phone footage (landscape + vertical) and edit reels in CapCut (free). Practice with one 30-second reel from an existing event.
> Learn: CapCut tutorials + YouTube: "smartphone videography basics"
> https://www.capcut.com/resource

### 15. Produce the club highlights reel — Dec 1–31 (5–6 hrs total)
Scoped for solo: a 2–3 minute highlights reel (not a full documentary) using your best footage and photos from Jul–Dec. Premiere it at a club meeting; post a 60-second cut online.
> Learn: CapCut — Video Editing Guide
> https://www.capcut.com/learn

### 16. [TEAM GROWTH] Recruit your first PR volunteer — Nov 2026 (1–2 hrs)
Announce at a club meeting: you need one photographer OR one writer, ~1 hr/week. Give them one clearly-defined recurring task (e.g. shoot every event).
> Learn: Asana — How to Delegate Effectively
> https://asana.com/resources/how-to-delegate

## Q3 — January–March 2027

*Reach: newsletter, website, district visibility — and building the committee.*

### 17. Learn Mailchimp (free tier) — Jan 1–15 (2–3 hrs)
Set up a free Mailchimp account, import member/partner emails (with permission), and build a newsletter template from your brand kit.
> Learn: Mailchimp 101 (official getting-started guides)
> https://mailchimp.com/resources/mailchimp-101/

### 18. Publish the first monthly newsletter — Jan 2027, then monthly (2 hrs/month)
Issue #1: recent projects, #MeetDebo highlight, upcoming events, one call-to-action. Keep it a 5-minute read. Reuse the template every month.
> Learn: Mailchimp — How to Write a Newsletter
> https://mailchimp.com/resources/how-to-write-a-newsletter/

### 19. Overhaul racdebo.org — Feb 1–Mar 15 (2 hrs/week for 6 weeks)
Rebuild the club website: home, about, projects (Hidden Ethiopia gallery), join us, and contact. Use a no-code builder or v0 so you can maintain it yourself.
> Learn: v0 by Vercel — build and publish a site by describing it
> https://v0.app

### 20. D9212 integration push — Jan–Mar, ongoing (1 hr/month)
Submit one club story per month to District 9212 newsletters and socials, tag district accounts in major posts, and connect with other clubs' PR directors for cross-promotion.
> Learn: Rotary — Promote Your Club (official PR resources)
> https://my.rotary.org/en/learning-reference/learn-topic/public-relations

### 21. [TEAM GROWTH] Document SOPs for every PR role — Feb 2027 (3–4 hrs total)
Write simple how-to docs (or record short videos) for each recurring task: posting workflow, template usage, newsletter steps, metrics logging. New members become productive in days, not months.
> Learn: Notion — How to Build a Team Wiki / SOPs
> https://www.notion.com/help/guides/how-to-build-a-wiki-for-your-company

### 22. [TEAM GROWTH] Fill 2–3 sub-committee roles — Mar 2027 (2–3 hrs)
Using your SOPs, onboard a Social Media Manager, Graphics Designer, and/or Editor from club members. Hand each one owned, recurring work — you shift from doing to directing.
> Learn: Rotaract Handbook — club committees (official guide)
> https://my.rotary.org/en/document/rotaract-handbook

---

## Q4 — April–June 2027

*Prove it: report the results and hand over a working system.*

### 23. Compile the full-year metrics — Apr 2027 (2–3 hrs)
Pull your monthly metrics sheet together: follower growth vs the 1,800 target, posting consistency, newsletter stats, district features, and events documented within 48 hrs.
> Learn: Buffer — Social Media Metrics That Matter
> https://buffer.com/library/social-media-metrics/

### 24. Design the Year-End PR Report — May 2027 (4–5 hrs)
A visual Canva report: goals vs results, best-performing content, budget spent vs the ETB 19,000–30,500 estimate, and recommendations for 2027/28. Present to the board; share highlights with the district.
> Learn: Canva — Free Report Templates
> https://www.canva.com/reports/templates/

### 25. [TEAM GROWTH] Prepare the handover pack + train your successor — Jun 1–20 (3–4 hrs)
Your PR HQ drive, SOPs, brand kit, passwords doc, and metrics sheet ARE the handover pack — that's why you built them. Walk your successor or committee through everything in 1–2 sessions.
> Learn: Rotary — Club Leadership Succession Planning
> https://my.rotary.org/en/learning-reference/learn-role/club-roles

### 26. Publish the year-in-review post — Jun 2027 (2 hrs)
Close the year publicly: a carousel or reel of the year's best moments, thank-yous to members and partners, and a teaser for 2027/28.
> Learn: Later — Instagram Carousel Best Practices
> https://later.com/blog/instagram-carousel/
