Strategic Public Image Roadmap and Transition Plan: Rotaract Club of Debo (District 9216, Rotary Year 2026/27)
Strategic Re-Alignment and District Redistricting Context
The public relations and strategic communication landscapes for the Rotaract Club of Debo undergo a foundational transition at the commencement of the 2026/27 Rotary Year1. Effective July 1, 2026, the historical borders of Rotary International District 9212 are formally redrawn, resulting in the creation of two separate administrative entities: District 9215 and District 92163. Under this redistricting framework, the Rotaract Club of Debo—established in late 2015 by students at Addis Ababa University to drive local community development and foster civic leadership—is positioned within District 92161.
This new district covers Ethiopia, Eritrea, and the Central, Eastern, Coastal, and Eastern Nairobi regions of Kenya, bringing together 78 Rotaract clubs and 83 Rotary clubs under a unified administrative structure3. During this first year of the district's operations, the public relations strategy for the Rotaract Club of Debo must shift from basic project reporting to a structured, cross-border visual narrative3.
Operating under the 2026/27 international theme "Create Lasting Impact," the club must adapt its public image collateral to align with District Governor Esayas Yeyesuswork's focus on structured governance and international fellowship3. The transition from the outgoing 2025/26 theme "Make It Happen" requires updating both digital and physical assets to ensure strict compliance with Rotary International's global brand standards5.

	Rotary International Visual Identity and Compliance Framework
Maintaining a unified global visual identity is essential for protecting the brand and ensuring that community and international service initiatives are instantly recognizable14. The Public Image committee of the Rotaract Club of Debo is responsible for ensuring all digital and print materials adhere strictly to the rules established by the Rotary International Board of Directors15.
The Masterbrand Signature
The Masterbrand Signature is the primary identifier for the club and combines the official Rotaract wheel with the "Rotaract" wordmark to its left14. Because the word "Rotaract" is a custom-designed wordmark, it cannot be replaced or recreated using standard computer typefaces14. The proportional relationship, alignment, and spacing between the wheel and the wordmark must remain unaltered14.
To maintain clean formatting, standard club logos must be surrounded by a buffer of clear space equal to or greater than the height of the capital letter "R" in the logo's wordmark14. A simplified version of the Masterbrand Signature, which omits the inner lines of the wheel, should be used for print sizes smaller than 1.25 centimeters (0.5 inches) or for detailed apparel embroidery14. The simplified logo requires a reduced clear space buffer equal to the height of the lowercase "o" in the wordmark17.
Standalone Elements and Sizing Limits
The standalone Rotaract wheel is designated as the Mark of Excellence and is reserved for high-impact visual placements14. When using the Mark of Excellence on public banners or collateral, a complete club-level Masterbrand Signature must appear nearby to provide context14. The standalone wheel must always feature the words "Rotaract International" within its borders, as there is no simplified version of this element14.
The standard wheel within a logo has a minimum printed size of 1.25 centimeters (0.5 inches)14. The standalone Mark of Excellence must be printed at a minimum height of 5.08 centimeters (2 inches)17. The wheel in simplified logo variations must never exceed 1.25 centimeters (0.5 inches) in digital or print layouts17.
